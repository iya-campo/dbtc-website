COMPUTER SCIENCE

The College Department opened in 1966 with 33 first year students who began their two-year associate diploma in electrical or mechanical technology. Government recognition for the complete five-year Mechanical Engineering (ME) and Electronics and Communications Engineering (ECE) degrees was obtained in 1973, hence the change of the name from Don Bosco Technical Institute to Don Bosco Technical College. Since then, the college department has achieved top-of- the-category and above-national-average performances in the board exams.

    COURSE DESCRIPTION

    Computer Science (BS CS)

    The BS in Computer Science(BSCS) program provides the students with the technical knowledge and skills necessary for specifying, evaluating and managing computer systems. The curriculum emphasizes a broad foundation in computer organization, software and mathematics, together with electives that enhance studentsâ€™ proficiency in various computer applications. Technical guidance is made available in such applications ranging from data processing to embedded systems, as well as in the analysis and design methodologies appropriate for hardware, software and firmware. A wide range of career opportunities exists for computer scientists, such as computer programming, systems analysis, database/network administration, computer-aided design/engineering, software engineering, computer graphics, internet applications development, computational science and hardware support.
